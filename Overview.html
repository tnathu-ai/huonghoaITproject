RMIT Infinitour

RMIT INFINITOUR

Topic
Motivation
Lanscape

The project we are working on is a mapping app for the students in our university called “RMIT Infinitour”. The phrase “Infinitour” stands for “infinitive tour”, and obviously, it is in the RMIT’s area. The main technology behind this app is AR (Augmented Reality), along with a little help of LPS (Local Positioning System), AI (Artificial Intelligence), and Cloud Computing.

“RMIT Infinitour” provides three main features for the users. The first one is “guiding”, which uses LPS to identify users’ location and gives instructions for them to go to that destination. The second function is “providing information” using AR technology. When the user holds their phone’s camera in front of a room or place in RMIT, some basic information of that place such as room ID, name, function, facts, etc. will pop up on the screen. Last but not least, the feature which comprises the “infinitive” part of the app’s name is “finding secret gifts”. There will be several “gifts” allocated in secret spots around the campus, and the user’s job is to find them, just like Pokémon Go. After collecting enough “gifts”, users can exchange those virtual gifts for real rewards such as lanyards, notebooks, etc. By including this feature, we hope that RMIT students can have a better knowledge of the university and spend some time to blow up some steam after long stressful hours of studying. 

Topic

We believe not only our team’s members, but most of the RMIT’s freshmen struggle in finding their way around the school in their first few weeks. We have heard numerous cases where students are late for class or even miss their tests due to being lost on the campus. Understand that difficulty, we create this app to help newbies to avoid any problems or inconveniences when starting their new journeys at RMIT. And to provide an even more interesting and attractive experience to the user, we include one of the newest technologies in today’s world – AR. As we know, the number of applications using AR has increased significantly in recent years and is expected to be the future of the world. This technology has been used in many fields to support humans, however, it is not so popular in Vietnam and people usually think of AR as something related to games than other things. Since “RMIT Infinitour'' using AR as the main technology, we believe it not only fits the most recent IT trends but also can bring AR closer to other people. Besides, as AR becomes more popular and wild used, having experience in this field will be an extreme advantage. If this app works successfully, not only we can prove our ability to work with AR to future employers, but also we are able to update the latest IT trends and work with various technologies.

Motivation

“RMIT Infinitour” is inspired by two extremely popular apps which are Google Maps and Pokémon Go, hence, it is not hard to point out some similarities between our product and these two. Although numerous applications are using AR in identifying objects or giving instructions, none of those are specifically designed for RMIT University like ours. The closest device which implements the same “mapping” function is the “static standing map” located on the 1st floor of building 1 in RMIT. It is a simple map that shows people what building they are in with no extra interactions. On the other hand, our app can provide a clearer illustration of the campus along with some helpful information; give step-by-step instructions for users to get to their destination, and contain interesting activities that entertain and stimulate users exploring the university. Therefore, “RMIT Infinitour” might not have to worry about competing with any opponents since there is no such thing as it.
