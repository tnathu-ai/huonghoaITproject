LiDAR Scanner

Hardware

A smartphone is necessary for this app because users have to download it via App Store and Play Store. Furthermore, your experience will be better if you own a smartphone that has a ToF sensor or LiDAR. In theory, their operating principle is the same; it is just different about how Apple and Android phone companies are called. With the ToF sensor or LiDAR, the software can detect an object's surface faster and accurately. In this case, when you move the phone to the room information plates at Building 1 and 2, the content can immediately pop-up without facing problems related to lag or not recognizing the object. However, you can still use a smartphone without this feature because the price for phones with ToF sensors or LiDAR is usually higher, and people rarely buy them.

The reason our team chooses VSC is that the app does not cost any money. This will save an amount of money for us, and we can use it to purchase other licensed apps for the project. Furthermore, we have used the VSC to develop a website for the previous assignment. This will make it easier to create a new website on the VSC one more time.

HTML can be defined as a frameset to create a website. We have completed the personal website, so we have basic knowledge about HTML. With HTML, we can upload the content to our website. Besides, some members who do not use it fluently can help each other find the difficulty and solve it. In this case, Thu is very good at HTML. If we have some problems with our codes, we can ask her for support.

JavaScript is one of the main three coding languages to develop a website. This language is nearly not too much complicated so that developers can learn and execute it efficiently (Ayush Gupta, 2019). It does not take much time for all members to get familiar with and understand the primary step in JavaScript. First, JS works on numerous browsers, platforms. Second, we can also use JS to develop the game in our project. As a consequence, we do not need to find more advanced coding languages to do it. Finally, JS is much faster and lighter than any other language. It ensures the website and our game will work smoothly without having lag.

We will use Unity 3D to create our AR app as well as a game. The price for the Personal version is free, so we do not have to pay anything. However, the provided resources will be limited, so the app is difficult to look professional. ARCore is a plugin from Google that has some exciting features that we need, such as Augmented Images, Instant Preview. It also allows us to create our 3D models and replace the original ones. Thu and Huy have little experience using Unity so that the remaining members can ask them for advice and solutions during the process.

Risks

Before the deadline:


For team member individually:
-        Technical issues: Our laptops could be exposed to viruses, spilled with water, or broken down before submitting our work, which resulted in re-writing everything and delaying the final deadline.
-        Health and family issues: Mental and physical health-related problems both have the same obstruction to our project. During a high time of deadlines like this, our members can have a breakdown due to the workload they take, eye soreness, headache, and other problems caused by working too much with the computer. Complications like these will hamper not only our project’s quality but also our wellbeing.
-        Lack of responsibility: One potential scenario is that someone will reduce their workload or intentionally not follow through with what was given to them. In the worst case, they ‘disappear.’ It could be that they drop the course or transfer to another institution. Either it is, other members will have to put extra time to cover their parts. The exhaustion from this will hurt our grades.
-        Timetable clash: Our different studying schedules can prevent us from arranging meetings and consultations. Because this is a team project, unification and interaction play a pivotal role in the final results. Hence the lack of communication does not do us any good.  

For the team generally:


-          Conflicts: Several conflicts could occur during the process. For example, misunderstanding, different points of view, disheartened feedback. These can make team members go against each other and refuse to cooperate, consequently disengage the team.
-          Lack of knowledge: Some tools we choose to use may be too difficult or require too much time to learn. Thus the project does not turn out correctly or has enough functions that we want.
-          Uneffective time distribution: We may spend too much time on one part while doing the others with only half the amount. The users will have a dissatisfying experience with this app, making it mediocre and slapdash.
-          Content incompletion: If each person writes their parts from their viewpoint without consulting other team members, there will be a lack of consistency in the content. Another possibility is when there is no one to make no one not spot the final check on our report, some errors or missing parts, thus lowering our grade.

After the deadline:


-          Late submission: This could happen due to individual’s problems like forgetting the time or the team’s faults by not communicating with each other.-          Dissatisfied outcome: We do not list all the functions needed; the app does not work well; our project receives low marks.

Testing

We will develop the app on iOS. First, this operating system usually brings the best experience and high security for their users (Mantra Malhotra, 2015). For testing our app, we have to scan all the QR codes of each classroom in Building 1 and 2 to ensure that the codes can work smoothly. Because the app also includes the AR feature so that we have to check the images and models. However, some problems related to visibility, such as lag, the displayed position, might happen. In this case, we do not think that we cannot fix every error of 100%. They are sometimes still incurred when AR technology is not yet at the best level of perfection.

Along with displaying the data of each classroom, the app must track in real-time so that students can keep tabs on their schedule. Furthermore, we have to confirm that the app's guiding system can lead the students to the campus's exact location. For example, a new student needs to attend his class on time; nevertheless, he does not know where the class is located. The app will help him by directing the way for him. It will provide the room number, floor, building, and expected time to get there. If all the facts mentioned in the app work fluently with as few errors as possible, the app completed state 1.

We will first make an alpha test on the team members' phones because our courses belong to the SST. It might be easier for us to check the data and bugs; consequently, we​ need to find the solution as soon as possible. Second, the app will be launched for the SST students as a beta test; we will collect their advice and comments to improve the app's quality. Third, the SBM and SCD students can use the app after that; this will be a challenge when their number has increased to more than a thousand people. Finally, we will officially release the app to all the students at RMIT. If there are any problems during the experience, they can give feedback on our official website to improve the quality and fix bugs.

If state 1 of our app is successful as we expected, we will develop the Android platform. This is a crucial step because the app can be more diverse. The most exciting section is turning the ARI into a game. Students can now explore the entire RMIT campus by collecting special items in-game and get a grand prize. It is very suitable for new students to go on a campus tour with a unique and engrossing experience.
