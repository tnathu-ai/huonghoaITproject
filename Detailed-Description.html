Aims

Transfering to a new place is terrifying. Some people require a very long time until they can finally get used to the total unfamiliarity. From that foundation, we came up with this project idea to serve one single aim, which is to help RMIT’s newbies to fit into a new environment. As freshmen ourselves, we understand how difficult and overwhelming things can get, especially for ones who come from another city or country. With our app, users can have a nice experience the moment they set foot on the campus. The app will lead them to their room, or take them for a campus tour with detailed description of each place. Having a better knowledge about our school beforehand will undoubtedly make an ease out of the fitting in progress.

Aims

Aside from that one aim, we have several goals that we wish to achieve:

-          Having high grades: I believe that not only our team but the others also make this to be one of their main goals. Grade is a type of recognition. Having high grades means that our project is guaranteed to be authentic and interesting. It works as a confirmation for our hard work and understanding, therefore will encourage us to strive harder in order to achieve further.

-          Having better knowledge on how to make a proper presentation: we are required to do a presentation for the assignment 3. In order to prepare for the presentation, there was a lecture held about this. There were some members in our team who had not known how to present our work in the most efficient way, but after the lecture, they improved significantly. Our goal when finishing this project is for everyone to be able to make the best presentation. 

-          Having deeper understanding on writing reports: including this assignment, we have been writing three reports so far. During these times, we also receive comments and feedback from both the teaching staff and our classmates. Their words have played an important part in the improvement of our reports. Whilst coding is an essential part of IT, writing reports is also just as important as that. On that account, we hope that after assignment 3, we understand how to write reports in the most professional and efficient manner. 

-          Connecting team members together to solve problems: teamwork plays a pivotal role in every career, especially in IT, where used to be thought of as an individual work. We have to cooperate with new people on new projects, sometimes we get along with each other well, and other times we do not. In a team with different personalities, different opinions, different timetables like ours, we have managed to work well with one another to produce a product that all of the team members are proud about. In the future, we hope that even if we have to work with people with contrasting personalities, we can put away all the confusions and collaborate well. 

Developing ideas

Before starting working on this project, we launched a meeting to choose which technology application we would do in our project. Although it seemed an easy job, we took quite a long time to decide because everyone had different ideas, and they all sounded cool. To deal with our first challenge, we had come to a solution: each member would have three days to research their ideas to persuade the other how interesting, compelling, possible, practical, and economical their thoughts were. After the time given, we had an online meeting to give short presentations about the IT application we desired to do. Through another lengthy discussion, all the members were convinced and agreed on Linh’s idea, which was an app using VR and AR to support students to go on a campus tour by themselves. The reason why we made this decision is that the motivation behind this app is close to what we had been through. We understand the difficulties that every RMIT newbie must experience, which is being lost in the school. It causes bunches of troubles such as late for class or exams, so we are ambitious to build this app.

00

Although we love the idea, there were not so many features in the app except for the “campus tour.” We were concerned that it was not enough to attract users for an extended period, so we continued to discuss additional features. After going through another long discussion, we summed up three possible features can be added to our app:

+ “giving instructions” for users to find a place and get there

+ creating a “virtual map” so that users do not need to walk around the campus, but they can still do the tour

+ a small AR game where users have to go around to collect items like Pokémon Go

However, Trang thought that the game was unnecessary because we did not encourage users to play it. Luckily, we immediately solved that concern with Thu’s idea: if users can collect a specific number of items, they can exchange them for real gifts such as lanyards or notebooks. Eventually, we could agree on the final features of our app.

Technologies and dividing tasks

Since we are in the first IT level, we have no experience working with VR and AR, particularly and building an app generally. In case of that, the first thing we did in our project is learning about these two technologies ourselves through online sources. Similar to what we had done previously, we decided that Thu, Trang, and Linh would work on AR, while Khiem and Huy found information about VR, then launched an online meeting to present to each other about crucial things we had learned. 

We encountered a piece of sad news at this stage: The tools and devices required to run a VR app exceeded our budget, so we had to remove VR from the project. However, we still kept the map but using AR instead. After unifying which tools and software we were going to use, we started distributing tasks to members. There were four main tasks: collecting information, taking pictures, designing the 3D graphic, and building the app. Since Huy had Khiem have the most robust AR foundation, they would make and develop the app. Meanwhile, Trang, Linh, and Thu were assigned to collect information, take pictures, and design, respectively. We have just finished stage 1 of the project and are planning to work on step 2 and phase 3 next week

01

Collecting information and taking photos

Since these two tasks relate to each other so Trang and Linh will do them together, their jobs are to walk around the school, discover every corner, take photos of every place, and gather all the necessary information about that place.

02

For instance, if they do this on building 1, Linh must capture the building at different aspects so that the app can recognize it wherever the user is standing, and Trang has to find useful information about the building such as “what rooms are there in the building?”, “how many floors does it have?”. Although it seems like just easy labor work, it consumes quite a lot of time and effort because RMIT is enormous.

Designing 3D models

Thu is allocated to design the 3D models of our app because she already had some basic CAD knowledge (Computer-Aided Design).

Her job is to design how the “information boxes,” “instruction box,” and “game’s items” will be looked like. If some objects are too complicated to be handled, she can find alternatives on the Internet as long as we design all the items. After that, Thu will put the information Trang has found into the models.

ROLE

Unlike many other teams, we did not define our tasks into roles like UI Designer or Website Developer. Due to the lack of time and skill, our team came to a conclusion that we would focus mainly on the report and presentation. For that reason, each person would be in charge of several sub- parts in the report and the whole team would work on the presentation later on. Because the parts were chosen willingly, no disheartenment or misunderstanding arose during the progress.
