Feedback

We will develop the app on iOS. First, this operating system usually brings the best experience and high security for their users (Mantra Malhotra, 2015). For testing our app, we have to scan all the QR codes of each classroom in Building 1 and 2 to ensure that the codes can work smoothly. Because the app also includes the AR feature so that we have to check the images and models. However, some problems related to visibility, such as lag, the displayed position, might happen. In this case, we do not think that we cannot fix every error of 100%. They are sometimes still incurred when AR technology is not yet at the best level of perfection.

Along with displaying the data of each classroom, the app must track in real-time so that students can keep tabs on their schedule. Furthermore, we have to confirm that the app's guiding system can lead the students to the campus's exact location. For example, a new student needs to attend his class on time; nevertheless, he does not know where the class is located. The app will help him by directing the way for him. It will provide the room number, floor, building, and expected time to get there. If all the facts mentioned in the app work fluently with as few errors as possible, the app completed state 

Scope and Limits 2

We will first make an alpha test on the team members' phones because our courses belong to the SST. It might be easier for us to check the data and bugs; consequently, we need to find the solution as soon as possible. Second, the app will be launched for the SST students as a beta test; we will collect their advice and comments to improve the app's quality. Third, the SBM and SCD students can use the app after that; this will be a challenge when their number has increased to more than a thousand people. Finally, we will officially release the app to all the students at RMIT. If there are any problems during the experience, they can give feedback on our official website to improve the quality and fix bugs.

If state 1 of our app is successful as we expected, we will develop the Android platform. This is a crucial step because the app can be more diverse. The most exciting section is turning the ARI into a game. Students can now explore the entire RMIT campus by collecting special items in-game and get a grand prize. It is very suitable for new students to go on a campus tour with a unique and engrossing experience.

“There's no such thing as perfection. You're never finished with a film. You run out of time.”-- Peter Jackson, director of `The Lord of the Rings’ and ‘The Hobbit’ trilogies

Communications play an important role in coming to the success of our group. First, we can relay the information to our teammates, this will help them to know their role as well as objectives during the process. Second, misunderstandings and delays cannot be avoided when working in a group, contacting each other for a care plan, providing a timetable to keep track will make the project proceeding better. Finally, difficulties might be incurred at any time so meetings need to be organized weekly, everyone can sit together and figure out the solution for the project (nutcache).

We will hold both online and offline meetings throughout the process of making our app. For the online meetings, everyone decided that Microsoft Teams would be the primary means of communication for meetings. The reason we chose it because of its convenience, we can make a video call or share our screen with another member so that they are able to check the codes and bugs, in addition, Teams works on any devices that we use so we can contact anywhere unless that place does not have the Internet connection (Bemo). Starting from week 9, online sessions will be held weekly on Friday via Teams, members must update their tasks and demonstrate how many that they have done, there will be troubleshooting at the end of the assembly additionally. Moreover, if someone does not have enough information or emergency issues, he or she can text other people in the group through Messenger to ask them, this creates diversity in communication, not necessarily forced to use Teams.

Before the Christmas break, we had a conversation between everyone to unify a project for Assignment 3, this was also our first online meeting. After the break, we have considered discussing the plan on Wednesday and Friday each week at RMIT. During the discussion, we can clarify each person's idea much better than the online one, this is also an opportunity for all teammates to cultivate, test the project and learn from each other. For example, we can share our codes and check if it can run smoothly on each laptop. We estimate until January 23rd, there will be 8 meetings organized, besides, on January 16th and 23rd, we have an extra conversation for all the members to double-check everything before the due date of Assignment 3.

However, we cannot always avoid issues in a group's communications, for example, one member does not attend the meeting due to personal reasons. The rules of the group allow one person to have the maximum of two absent days , the reason we do that is because it is difficult to oblige everyone participating in all the meetings. This also includes online meetings, if they miss more than two days, we will examine the contribution rating of that member and decrease it. In addition, we have to send a message or call them to remind them. But we hope that everyone can manage their time appropriately so they will attend in all the meetings.

Skills and jobs

AR developer

Job description
   This is the most essential position in our project since our app is mostly based on AR. As the name implies, his or her job is to build an app that can censor an object and show a virtual graphic on the screen. Additionally, an AR developer also has to improve the accuracy and sensitivity of the app as well as the smoothness of users’ experiences. He or she must make sure that their app can work perfectly in different environments and conditions. Lastly, they need to think of new features and update older features in order to prevent users from boredom. The quality of our app and how long we can keep our users considerably depend on our AR developer.

Required skills
-       Creativity
-   Have a deep foundation of programming languages such as C#, C++, JavaScript, etc.
-   Be able to use SDK
-       Problem-solving
-   Graphic designing

UI and UX designer

Job description
   The user is an indispensable part in every application or website on the market. There is no use in creating a website where no one has understanding on how it works or what its functions are. Therefore, UI/UX designers are first- handedly searched for in my group.

   UI/UX designers need to be able to understand users’ thoughts and perpetually hypothesize their viewpoint is also the users so that the opinions are subjective and right to the point. They also need to be in the user’s shoes in order to bring out a product that puts the users’ experience on top.



Required skills
-          Understanding some programming languages like HTML5, JavaScript, CSS and so on.
-          Good communication and collaboration skill
-          User empathy
-          Curiosity
-          UI prototyping
-          Business analytical skill

Marketing Operator

Job description
   Since we are just a small team with no reputation, therefore, it is difficult to have a huge number of users in the first place. In order to expand our market and get more users to our app, a marketing operator is irreplaceable. Taking this position means that he or she has to make plans, think of strategies, devise campaigns, research about markets, design advertisements, contact cooperators, etc. In other words, a marketing operator has to do anything to introduce our app to new users, maintain publicity and attract new investors and cooperators.




Required skills
-       Creativity
-   Planning and organizing
-   Good Writing skill
-       Designing skill
-       Researching skill
-   Event organizing
-       Communication skill
