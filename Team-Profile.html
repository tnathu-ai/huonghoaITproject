Career Plans

For the first assignment, we had explained in detail about our ideal type of job. In summary, Trang’s dream job was security manager, Khiem’s was mobile software engineer, Huy’s was project manager, Thư’s was business analyst and mine was C++ Developer. None of our career plans is the same because of the difference in each person’s personality and skills. Trang and Huy both want to be a manager but in different fields as one is invested more on security whilst the other is fond of taking care of the whole project. Khiem loves playing video games, that is why it is easily understandable that he would like to have a career in the mobile app market. Thư has always shown affection towards data and business. More than once she has displayed her interest in having a business of her own. For that reason, I believe becoming a business analyst is most suitable for her. For my part, I have been drawn to like a programming language called C++ from the first time I was introduced to it. The language is not easy to handle, specifically for someone who is a slow learner like me. However, challenges are what make you grow and I believe that the process will make me develop better as a programmer. All in all, despite having diverse ideal jobs in the technology field, there is one thing we have in common, which is passion and hard-work. 

Group processes

All the tasks were divided based on each person’s timetable and proficiency. Hence no overlap or discontent happened during the whole time. Throughout assignment 3, our five members have contributed wholeheartedly to bring what initially was just a concept into a reality. However, we can not say that things were going 100 percent right for our team. Like any other group, we have to face schedule clashes, and it plays a part in slowing down our progress. If the assignment is to be extended, and unexpected issues happen during the time, there is a high chance that we will not be able to finish this on time.
